Geophysics Overview

Geophysics is a multidisciplinary field using physical principles to investigate Earth's interior. Techniques like seismic, magnetic, gravity, and remote sensing methods are employed for studying subsurface structures, geological formations, and environmental phenomena. Geophysicists contribute to understanding Earth's composition, tectonic processes, and resource distribution, impacting mineral exploration, environmental monitoring, infrastructure development, and hazard assessment.

Remote Sensing

Definition: Collecting Earth surface and atmospheric data from a distance using sensors on satellites or other platforms.

Applications: Remote sensing aids in agriculture, forestry, environmental science, urban planning, disaster management, and climate studies. It provides essential tools for large-scale data collection, monitoring, and decision-making regarding natural resources.

Applied Geophysics

Definition: Applying physical principles to address practical problems in various industries and applications.

Applications: Applied geophysics plays a crucial role in environmental studies, civil engineering, mineral exploration, and natural resource management. It is employed for mapping subsurface structures, locating groundwater resources, assessing soil composition, and identifying mineral or hydrocarbon deposits.

Environmental Geophysics

Definition: Applying geophysical methods to study and analyze the Earth's subsurface concerning environmental issues.

Applications: Environmental geophysics is crucial for environmental site assessments, pollution studies, landfill monitoring, and detecting subsurface features related to industrial activities. It aids in understanding the spatial distribution of contaminants, assessing remediation efforts, and informing decision-making for sustainable environmental management.
